Indications and Usage

BELEODAQ is a histone deacetylase inhibitor indicated for the treatment of patients with relapsed or refractory peripheral T-cell lymphoma (PTCL). This indication is approved under accelerated approval based on tumor response rate and duration of response. An improvement in survival or disease-related symptoms has not been established. Continued approval for this indication may be contingent upon veriﬁcation and description of clinical beneﬁt in the conﬁrmatory trial.

Duration of Exposure and Incidence of Adverse Events (AEs)

AEs occurring in ≥10% of patients (N = 129) with R/R PTCL (NCI-CTCAE grades 1 to 4)1

  • Most patients (113 of 129) remained on the target dose (1000 mg/m2)1,2
    • Dosage adjustments due to AEs occurred in 16 of 129 patient treated with BELEODAQ

  • The relative dose intensity (doses administered vs planned) was 98%2

  • Treatment duration ranged from 1 to 33 cycles (median, 2 cycles)1,2

  • Twenty-five patients (19.4%) discontinued treatment with BELEODAQ due to AEs1
    • The AEs reported most frequently as the reason for discontinuation of treatment included anemia, febrile neutropenia, fatigue, and multi-organ failure

  • Treatment-emergent AEs (TEAEs) occurred in 97% of patients and were generally mild to moderate in severity2
    • The most common AEs were nausea, fatigue, pyrexia, anemia, and vomiting

  • Grade 3/4 AEs occurring in ≥5% of patients (N = 129) were anemia (11%), thrombocytopenia (7%), dyspnea (6%), and fatigue (5%)1

  • 47% (n = 61) experienced serious AEs (SAEs)1,2
    • No clinically meaningful difference in SAEs was observed in patients based on age (<65 years vs <65 years or <75 years vs ≥75 years)

NCI-CTCAE, National Cancer Institute Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events. Note: AEs are listed by order of incidence in the all-grades category first, then in the grades 3 to 4 category; measured by NCI-CTCAE version 3.0.

Tolerability Established With the BELIEF Trial1,2

Most patients tolerated the target dose of BELEODAQ1,2

12% OF PATIENTS receiving BELEODAQ required a dosage reduction due to AEs

88% OF PATIENTS receiving BELEODAQ did not require a dosage reduction due to AEs

In studies, BELEODAQ® (belinostat) has not been associated with clinically relevant changes in heart rate or PR- or QRS-interval duration2

BELEODAQ is the only agent that the FDA has approved for the treatment of R/R PTCL that was studied in patients (N = 120) with platelet counts down to 50,000/μL2

  • R/R PTCL patients with platelet counts <100,000/μL are typically ineligible for clinical trials3

1. BELEODAQ® [Prescribing Information], Acrotech Biopharma, LLC; January 2020. 2. O’Connor O, et al. J Clin Oncol. 2015;33:2492-2499. 3. Savage KJ, et al. Blood. 2014:124:3075. doi.org/10.1182/blood.V124.21.3075.3075.
