BELEODAQ Is a Pan-HDAC Inhibitor FDA Approved for R/R PTCL

Although the exact MOA of HDAC inhibitors is still being studied, BELEODAQ is believed to work as follows:

  1. HDACs catalyze the removal of acetyl groups from the lysine residues of histones, which promotes a closed chromatin structure. HDACs also remove acetyl groups from some nonhistone proteins7,8

  2. In vitro, BELEODAQ inhibits the enzymatic activity of HDACs at nanomolar concentrations (<250 nM). This causes accumulation of acetylated histones and nonhistone proteins, which can promote an open chromatin structure1,8

  3. BELEODAQ induces cell-cycle arrest and/or apoptosis of some transformed cells. BELEODAQ shows preferential cytotoxicity toward tumor cells compared to normal cells1

HDAC, histone deacetylase; MOA, mechanism of action; R/R PTCL, relapsed/refractory peripheral T-cell lymphoma.
